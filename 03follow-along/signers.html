Who were the Signers

Famous Signers

Some of the most famous signers of the Declaration of Independence include

John Hancock
President of the Continental Congress, known for his famously large signature, which has become synonymous with the word "signature" itself.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration, later the 3rd President of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
Statesman, inventor, and diplomat; one of the most celebrated figures of the founding era.
John Adams
A leading advocate for independence, later the 2nd President. He and Jefferson famously both died on July 4, 1826 — exactly 50 years after the Declaration was adopted.
Roger Sherman
The only person to sign all four of the major founding documents: the Articles of Association, the Declaration, the Articles of Confederation, and the Constitution.
Robert Livingston
A member of the Committee of Five that drafted the Declaration, though he never actually signed it (he was recalled to New York before signing day).
Samuel Adams
A key revolutionary organizer and cousin of John Adams, instrumental in building colonial resistance to British rule.

These men risked their lives, fortunes, and reputations by signing — the document was considered an act of treason against the British Crown.
